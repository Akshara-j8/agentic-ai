# Escalation Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines when frontline support must hand a case to a human specialist or supervisor.

## Clauses

**ES-1 Legal Language.** Always escalate when the customer mentions legal action, attorney, lawsuit, regulator, subpoena, chargeback threat, or formal complaint.

**ES-2 Complaint Language.** Always escalate when the customer asks to file a complaint, report the company, contact corporate, or receive a written final response.

**ES-3 Safety Issues.** Escalate any claim involving injury, unsafe product behavior, fire, contamination, or property damage.

**ES-4 High-Value Refunds.** Refunds over $50 require human approval under RF-4 before the agent promises an outcome.

**ES-5 Repeated Contacts.** Escalate when a customer has contacted support three or more times about the same unresolved issue.

**ES-6 Security Incidents.** Escalate account takeover, unauthorized order, or suspicious access reports under AS-6.

**ES-7 Escalation Notes.** Include the trigger phrase, order ID if available, summary of prior actions, and requested resolution.

**ES-8 No Admission.** Agents should acknowledge frustration but must not admit legal liability or policy violation.
